# What is Plakk?

Plakk is a utility for moving snippets between your own devices without
emailing yourself, messaging yourself, or opening another app just to hand
something off.

The product is built around a simple idea: when you have text, a link, a file,
an image, or any other snippet on one device, adding it to Plakk should be the
fastest way to make it available on your other devices.

Plakk is not a social sharing tool, a team workspace, or a clipboard history
app. It is a personal cross-device handoff utility.

## Why Plakk exists

People who work across multiple devices regularly need to move small pieces of
content between them. The common workarounds are awkward: email yourself, send
yourself a message, upload a file manually, or rely on platform-specific tools
that only work in one ecosystem.

Plakk should remove that friction by giving each platform a low-effort way to
add a snippet:

- on desktop, through a toolbar drop target;
- on mobile, through a custom keyboard;
- on the web, through a simple interface.

Once added, the snippet is stored in the user's linked storage provider, such as
Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox, and becomes available from their other Plakk
surfaces.

The product should feel like a quiet utility: fast to access, easy to understand,
and mostly out of the way. The user should not feel like they are managing a new
workspace. They should feel like they have a reliable place to put something
when they need it on another device.

## Audience

Plakk can be used by anyone, but it is most useful for power users who work
across multiple platforms. Because Plakk works on web, mobile, and desktop,
including Linux, it is designed for people who do not live inside one device or
one ecosystem.
