# Use readable mirrors and online-only mutations

Plakk has one authoritative remote source and device-owned readable mirrors. Each desktop device durably retains the last-confirmed current account and linked storage provider, all authoritative snippet records including their upload status, and automatically selected managed file content; snapshot, cursor, pull, and wake processing refresh that mirror after reconnect. Cached display facts and local files remain readable without online capability, but they never become a competing mutation source. Explicit sign-out purges that account's readable mirror, staged recovery content, managed files, and credentials from the device.

Adding and deleting snippets are online-only commands. Before creating any authoritative record, the originating device atomically stages and verifies stable source content with a durable recovery record. Once provider transfer is ready to begin, it creates an authoritative `UPLOADING` snippet so every device can display it, extends a backend heartbeat deadline while transferring content, reports failure when possible, and finalizes the provider object as `UPLOADED` on success. Once Electron main accepts local preparation, that activity appears in the origin's local state but is not synchronized to other devices. Origin failure or heartbeat expiry produces `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`; a conclusive provider rejection or transfer failure produces `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`; and failure after a confirmed provider upload during backend finalization produces `FINALIZATION_FAILED`. Devices synchronize these upload states through the same change feed as other authoritative snippet changes.

If backend creation returns an error after staging, no authoritative snippet or upload status exists. Electron main retains origin-local Upload activity and presents `Upload failed` with Retry and Remove; Retry reattempts backend creation from the staged bytes, while Remove discards the local staged content. `Interrupted` is reserved for process exit while preparation, backend creation, or transfer is still in flight. Pre-authoritative Upload activity is visible to the origin's desktop surfaces but never synchronizes to other devices.

`CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`, `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`, and `FINALIZATION_FAILED` are stable. The snippet remains visible on every device until the user explicitly retries or deletes it; the backend does not hide or remove it automatically after another timeout. This keeps failed cross-device work honest and recoverable instead of making it silently disappear.

Primary UI uses concise labels rather than status enums or diagnostic sentences: `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED` is `Upload failed`, `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED` is `Storage issue`, and `FINALIZATION_FAILED` is `Couldn't finish`, each with a cross icon. On the origin, a durable local recovery record proving process exit overrides `Upload failed` with `Interrupted`. The origin exposes Retry and Delete, while other devices expose only Delete; detailed causes remain secondary.

Every device may delete a `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`, `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`, or `FINALIZATION_FAILED` snippet, but only the originating device may retry while it retains the staged source content and recovery metadata. Retrying `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED` or `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED` starts a new provider transfer from the verified staged bytes. Retrying `FINALIZATION_FAILED` transfers no bytes: the origin reuses the provider object reference persisted immediately after provider success and invokes the idempotent backend completion command again. The authoritative status remains `FINALIZATION_FAILED` during this request and changes directly to `UPLOADED` on success; only the origin's local state shows temporary retry activity. Other devices expose no retry action, and discarding the origin's recovery content leaves deletion followed by adding the source again as the remaining recovery.

The live origin attempt automatically makes a bounded number of retries for transient backend, network, or provider failures while upload status remains `UPLOADING`. Exhausted connection loss and non-recoverable origin errors become `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`; only a terminal error reported by the linked provider becomes `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`; and backend finalization errors after confirmed provider success become `FINALIZATION_FAILED`. Exact schedules and attempt counts are implementation constants rather than synchronized product state.

The backend enforces ownership of upload recovery with a client-generated capability secret scoped to that snippet's origin. It stores only the secret's hash and requires both account authentication and the capability for provider preparation, heartbeat, explicit failure, commit, cancellation, and retry. Electron main stores the capability securely with the durable recovery record; it is never sent to renderers or synchronized to other devices. Retry rotates the live attempt generation so late commands from an earlier attempt are rejected.

Normal account-level deletion is rejected while a snippet is `UPLOADING`. The live attempt holder alone may cancel the upload; after status becomes `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`, `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`, `FINALIZATION_FAILED`, or `UPLOADED`, any authenticated device on the account may delete the snippet.

Cancellation is removal. The backend authoritatively deletes the `UPLOADING` snippet, publishes that deletion to every device, invalidates the attempt capability, and rejects late heartbeats or finalization. It does not preserve an intentionally withdrawn upload as failed; origin failure and heartbeat expiry remain paths to `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`.

Cleanup of provider upload sessions or objects left by `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED`, `STORAGE_UPLOAD_FAILED`, or canceled uploads is best-effort. The origin aborts active transfer and Plakk immediately removes known provider content when possible, while incomplete provider sessions may expire according to provider behavior. A confirmed provider object referenced by `FINALIZATION_FAILED` is retained for zero-byte Retry rather than cleaned up. Cleanup failure is logged and measured but does not delay authoritative failure or removal; Plakk adds no durable cleanup workflow unless observed orphaning later justifies one.

Deleting a `FINALIZATION_FAILED` or `UPLOADED` snippet with a known provider object follows the same non-blocking rule. The online backend command first removes the authoritative snippet and publishes its deletion, then attempts to delete the provider object as a best-effort side effect. Provider deletion failure never restores the snippet or delays the UI; orphaned provider content is accepted for now and measured rather than introducing a cleanup queue.

This synchronized upload lifecycle does not make mutation work offline-capable. Plakk persists verified staged content and a minimal recovery record, but has no queued background processing or automatic restart resume. Closing a window leaves Electron main uploading, while explicit Quit warns that upload is active. If the user confirms, Plakk reports `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED` when possible, preserves local recovery, and exits; a crash is resolved as `CLIENT_UPLOAD_FAILED` by heartbeat expiry. On reopening, the origin shows Interrupted with explicit Retry instead of restarting work. Deletion remains an online command; the invoking screen may hide the snippet optimistically, but the readable mirror and other windows change only after authoritative confirmation. Command failure rolls back that one screen from the unchanged local state, with no durable optimistic deletion journal.

While an upload is active or recoverably interrupted, Electron main includes confirmed upload activity in the device's local state. Every desktop surface displays that shared local activity and recovery action, while a screen-local optimistic overlay may temporarily exist only on the invoking screen and other devices display only authoritative upload status. Active transfer state is process-local; Interrupted and its recovery record persist across application exit but are not synchronized.

Upload UI is deliberately indeterminate. The invoking screen may show a spinner immediately during optimistic local preparation; confirmed origin-device activity then comes from Electron main, while other devices begin showing a spinner only after the authoritative status becomes `UPLOADING`. No surface displays byte percentages. Heartbeats communicate liveness to the backend but do not publish progress changes through the change feed.

Online commands may use standard screen-local optimistic presentation. The renderer screen that invokes Add, Retry, Cancel, or Delete may immediately show the expected transient result, but does not broadcast it to Home, Tray, or another window and does not write it into the readable mirror. Once Electron main confirms durable local staging or receives the authoritative backend result, its local state replaces the overlay and broadcasts real device state. Failure discards the overlay and shows local command feedback; no optimistic state survives restart or becomes an offline mutation queue.

Every snippet continues to reference complete file content in the user's linked storage provider. Presentation remains derived from file name and content rather than authoritative kind, title, or content-type metadata.

Automatic file mirroring uses one strict, age-independent rule: `UPLOADED` content smaller than 1 GiB (`1,073,741,824` bytes) is hydrated on every device, while content of exactly 1 GiB or larger is not automatically hydrated. Every authoritative snippet record remains in the readable mirror regardless of content size.

Snippet records and managed file content are synchronized separately. Every device stores every authoritative record locally. For `UPLOADED` files below 1 GiB it also hydrates the content automatically; for larger files it stores only the record until the user invokes Download. That device then shows an indeterminate downloading state and enables Copy only after the managed content becomes available.

Each automatic or manual hydration request makes bounded retries for transient failures. Exhaustion sets device-local content availability to failed, stops further automatic attempts for that request, and exposes an explicit Retry action; it never changes authoritative upload status. An explicit Retry starts a new bounded hydration request.

Stable local content availability and download failure are persisted with the device's local Snippet state. A failed download therefore remains failed across application restart and performs no background work until the user explicitly retries; deleting the authoritative Snippet removes its local availability state and managed content. Active downloading remains process-local rather than a durable work queue.

If the application exits or crashes during hydration, partial temporary content is discarded and the persisted local state becomes interrupted rather than resuming silently. On reopening, the primary UI still uses a cross icon, `Interrupted`, and Retry; detailed diagnostics remain secondary and do not replace this concise presentation. Interrupted upload uses the same concise presentation and explicit-retry rule but retains its verified staged source, while explicit Cancel remains authoritative removal.

Persisted Available state is never trusted without managed-content validation. Missing, partial, or corrupt content is invalidated and never served. `UPLOADED` content below 1 GiB begins a fresh bounded automatic hydration; content at or above 1 GiB returns to the manual Download action. Failed automatic recovery uses the same concise device-local failure and Retry presentation.

The threshold governs automatic mirroring rather than limiting local storage. A user may manually download content of exactly 1 GiB or larger while online, after which that device retains it until the snippet is deleted. Before managed content is available, the snippet remains visible with a download action and cannot be copied; downloading state and failure remain device-local. Initial launch includes neither per-snippet local removal nor device-wide cache clearing; cache-management UI is deferred until post-launch evidence justifies it.

Successful upload also counts as explicit local retention on the originating device. After backend commit, Electron main atomically adopts the verified staged source into managed snippet content regardless of size, rather than discarding it and downloading the same provider content again.
